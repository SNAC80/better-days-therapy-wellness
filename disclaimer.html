Crisis Resources

If you are in a mental health emergency, please call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), call 911, or go to your nearest emergency room immediately. Text HOME to 741741 for the Crisis Text Line.

1. No Therapist-Client Relationship

The information provided on this website is for general informational purposes only. Visiting this website, submitting a contact form, or reading any content on this Site does not establish a therapist-client relationship between you and Better Days Therapy & Wellness, PLLC or Dr. Stephanie Booker. A therapeutic relationship is only established through a formal intake process and signed consent forms.

2. Not Medical or Mental Health Advice

The content on this website is not intended to be, and should not be construed as, medical advice, mental health diagnosis, or treatment recommendations. Always seek the advice of a qualified mental health professional regarding any questions you may have about a mental health condition or treatment.

3. Not a Crisis Service

Better Days Therapy & Wellness, PLLC is not a crisis service. We are unable to guarantee immediate response to messages submitted through this website. If you are experiencing a mental health crisis, please use the emergency resources listed above.

4. Telehealth Limitations

Telehealth services are subject to licensing restrictions. Dr. Stephanie Booker is licensed to provide telehealth services only to clients physically located in Texas or Illinois at the time of the session. It is the client's responsibility to ensure they are located in a state where Dr. Booker is licensed to practice.

5. Results Not Guaranteed

Therapy outcomes vary significantly from person to person. No guarantee of specific results is made or implied. The effectiveness of therapy depends on many factors, including the nature of the presenting concerns, the client's engagement and commitment, and other individual circumstances.

6. Confidentiality Exceptions

While all therapy sessions are confidential, there are legally mandated exceptions to confidentiality, including but not limited to: imminent risk of harm to self or others, suspected child abuse or neglect, elder abuse, and court orders. These exceptions will be explained in detail during the informed consent process at the start of treatment.
